01

PROBLEM

⚡
Overcurrent Risk

DC motors can experience stall conditions or overheating, leading to excessive current draw, thermal damage, and potential fire risk.

🎯
Objective

The objective of this project was to design and implement a low-cost embedded system capable of detecting overcurrent and overtemperature conditions and safely shutting down the motor to prevent further damage.

Motor driver disabled on fault detection

03

KEY ENGINEERING DECISIONS

Component Selection

Motor

5V DC Motor chosen to demonstrate stall behaviour

ACS712

Current Sensor selected for electrical isolation and simplicity

DS18B20

Temperature Sensor chosen for analogue to digital accuracy

L298N

Motor Driver used for prototyping and controlling motor speed through PWM

Design Rationale

5k ohm pull up resistor used between the temperature sensor and Arduino for One wire communication

Fault condition latched to prevent auto restart after failure

PWM control used to regulate motor speed

REFLECTION AND IMPROVEMENTS

Future improvements would include replacing the L298N motor driver for a MOSFET driver for improved efficiency. Utilising a manufactured and designed PCB instead of a breadboard and Arduino for wiring to create higher organisation and minimise space.
